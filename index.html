About Me

I am an AI & Machine Learning researcher with a PhD in computer science (Nov 2023), specialized in healthcare applications. My doctoral research, conducted in partnership with Amiens University Hospital, focused on motion analysis using artificial intelligence — particularly applied to Parkinson’s disease.

I am currently a post-doctoral researcher at the University of Picardie Jules Verne, working on the RAUC project: an optimized care pathway for digestive surgical emergencies, using AI to predict post-operative complications. Previously, I led the AI & Data Science workstream at Capgemini Engineering on a generative AI medical assistant demonstrator, coordinating a team of 15–20 people.

In parallel, I’m founding Noovate-lab, an AI-first studio building practical products for healthcare and SMBs. I bring deep expertise in LLM-based systems, multimodal RAG, computer vision (medical imaging), explainable AI, and production deployment of ML pipelines.
